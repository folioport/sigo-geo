Map examples

Census population analysis. British identity in Scottish cities.

A wealth of spatial data is generated by the UK census. This example was inspired by the Scottish referendum. In cities most people identify as Scottish rather than British, although in some neighbourhoods there are exceptions. This is influenced by migration levels and the presence of universities.

Methods and data

Includes Census and Ordnance Survey data. Crown copyright and database rights (2015)

Greenspace in Scottish cities. Comparing population size and green cover.

Highlighting the greenspaces present within Scottish cities, showing total cover and resident population. A three kilometer radius was used to compare between the cities. I used an infographic inspired design to display the maps and associated data.

Methods and data

Includes Ordnance Survey and Forestry Commission data. Crown copyright and database rights (2015)

Peat soils conservation. Forestry plantations on carbon rich peat soils.

Produced as part of the EcoCo LIFE + project in central Scotland. This map identifies timber plantations on carbon rich deep peat soils, where conservation activites such as tree removal could benefit peatland habitats.

Methods and data

Includes Ordnance Survey, Scottish Natural Heritage, Forestry Commission data. Crown copyright and database rights (2015)
